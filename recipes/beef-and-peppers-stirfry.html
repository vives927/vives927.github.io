Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef (ribeye or sirloin), thinly sliced
  • 1-1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp rice wine, optional
  • 2 tsp corn starch
  • 2 tbsp peanut oil
  • 1 medium green pepper, sliced into half-inch strips
  • 1 medium red pepper, sliced into half-inch strips
  • 1 onion sliced
  • 1 tbsp ginger minced
  • 2 cloves garlic minced

For the sauce:

  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/2 c chicken stock
  • 3 tbsp black bean sauce
  • 1 tbsp corn starch
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Instructions

  1. In a shallow bowl, combine beef with soy sauce, rice wine (if using), and corn starch and toss together, set aside.
  2. In a small mixing bowl, combine all the sauce ingredients.
  3. Heat 1 tbsp of oil in a wok or a heavy duty skillet over high heat until smoke comes up. Add the beef and stir-fry quickly, about 1-2 minutes until the center of beef slices is slightly pink. Transfer to another plate and set aside.
  4. Add the remaining oil in a wok and stir-fry peppers, onion, and ginger until crisp tender, about 2-3 minutes. Add garlic and stir-fry.
  5. Return the beef to the wok and toss. Pour the sauce over and toss everything together until the sauce thickens. Serve hot with rice.
