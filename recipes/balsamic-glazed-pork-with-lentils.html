Ingredients

  • 1 cup green lentils, rinsed
  • kosher salt and black pepper
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1.25-lb pork tenderloin
  • 1 red apple, cut into 1‚ÅÑ2-inch pieces
  • 1 celery stalk, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh flat-leaf parsley leaves
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice

Instructions

  1. Heat oven to 400° F. Bring 4 cups water to a boil.
  2. Add the lentils and 1 tsp salt and simmer, stirring occasionally, until tender, 20 to 30 minutes. Drain and rinse under cold water to cool.
  3. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine the balsamic vinegar and brown sugar.
  4. Heat 1 tbsp of the oil in a large ovenproof skillet over medium-high heat. Season the pork with 1/4 tsp each salt and pepper. Cook, turning occasionally, until browned, 6 to 8 minutes.
  5. Transfer the pork to oven. Roast until cooked through, 10 to 12 minutes, basting with the glaze twice during the last 5 minutes. Let rest before slicing.
  6. In a medium bowl, toss cooked lentils with the apple, celery, parsley, lemon juice, remaining 2 tbsp oil, and 1/4 tsp each salt and pepper. Serve with the pork and any pan drippings.
