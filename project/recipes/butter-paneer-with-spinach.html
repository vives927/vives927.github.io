Butter Paneer with Spinach

Back to Recipes
Category:  Main Dish
Date Published: 11/05/2014
Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 medium onion, sliced (about 1 cup)
  • 1 (3-inch) knob ginger, chopped (about 3 tbsp)
  • 1 jalapeno pepper, sliced
  • Kosher salt
  • 2 14.5-oz cans fire-roasted diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 3 tbsp honey
  • 1/4 to 1/2 cup heavy cream, to taste
  • 2 tbsp crushed fenugreek leaves, toasted (optional)
  • 1 bunch spinach blanched, drained, and chopped (or 10 oz frozen spinach, thawed and drained)
  • 1 lb paneer, diced into small cubes
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro leaves

Instructions

  1. In a medium saucepan, melt butter over medium heat, then stir in onions, ginger, and jalapeno. Sprinkle with 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt and cook, stirring occasionally, until onions soften and just begin to take on color, 5 to 7 minutes.
  2. Add canned tomatoes, cinnamon, and honey. Cook uncovered, stirring occasionally, until sauce is thickened with only a little liquid poking through the tomatoes and onions, 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. Transfer sauce to blender (or use an immersion blender) and carefully puree on high speed until very smooth, about 1 minute. Wipe saucepan clean and pour sauce through a fine mesh strainer back into pot. Add salt and cream to taste, and fenugreek if using.
  4. Stir spinach into sauce, then fold in paneer, taking care not to break up the cubes of cheese. Let spinach and paneer warm through for 5 minutes. Stir in half of cilantro, sprinkle with remaining cilantro, and serve. Serve over rice.
