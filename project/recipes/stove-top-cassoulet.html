Stove-Top Cassoulet

Back to Recipes
Category:  Main Dish
Date Published: 11/05/2014
Servings: 8

Ingredients

  • 2 tsp olive oil
  • 1 lb skinless chicken thigh, trimmed of all visible fat, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 lb kielbasa, reduced-fat, turkey, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 medium Leek, cleaned and chopped
  • 2 medium carrots, chopped
  • 1 medium apple, Granny Smith, peeled and chopped
  • 1 14.5-oz can diced tomatoes
  • 2 cups reduced-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 2 14.5-oz cans cannellini beans, rinsed and drained
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 1/8 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp parsley, fresh, chopped
  • 3 tsp lemon zest, grated

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tsp of the oil in a nonstick Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook, turning occasionally, until browned, about 6 minutes; transfer to bowl.
  2. Add the remaining 1 tsp of oil to the same Dutch oven, then add the kielbasa. Cook over medium-high heat until browned, about 4 minutes.
  3. Add 2 of the garlic cloves, the leek, carrots, and apple to the kielbasa in the Dutch oven. Cook, stirring, 1-2 minutes. Return the chicken to the Dutch oven. Add the tomatoes, broth, wine, beans, sugar, allspice, and bay leaf; bring to a boil. Reduce the heat and simmer, covered, until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender, about 25 minutes.
  4. Combine the parsley, lemon zest, and remaining 1 chopped garlic clove in a small bowl; set aside. Discard bay leaf. Sprinkle the cassoulet with the parsley mixture just before serving. Yields generous 1 cup per serving.
